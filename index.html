Yale Hackathon

2nd place

A quantum optimizer for insurance portfolio selection, developed at Yale's YQuantum for the Capgemini and The Hartford challenge. We reduced 50 assets to 8 sectors mapped onto 8 qubits and applied QAOA to identify the optimal allocation under real regulatory constraints.

Circuit-depth optimizations roughly halved the depth and raised simulated accuracy from 17% to 53%, and we validated the result against a brute-force classical solve. Placed 2nd of 35 teams and won Best Computational Accuracy.

  • Python
  • Bloqade
  • QAOA
  • QUBO

Fall 2025

A training attachment that clamps onto a track hurdle and swings a padded arm into position for trail-leg drills. Designed in SolidWorks, with the loaded arm validated in FEA and refined across several prototype iterations. The mechanism uses a spring-loaded, ball-bearing pivot that returns the arm after each strike.

Wind Tunnel (NACA 2415)

Spring 2026

Characterized a NACA 2415 airfoil in a subsonic wind tunnel across seven angles of attack. Surface-pressure data was acquired through LabVIEW and reduced in Python to compute lift and moment coefficients, then benchmarked against vortex-panel-method predictions and published NACA data. Diagnosed and corrected two faulty pressure taps to preserve data integrity.

  • Aerodynamics
  • LabVIEW
  • Python
  • Data Analysis
