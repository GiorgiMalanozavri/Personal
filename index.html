Yale Hackathon

2nd place

A quantum optimizer for insurance portfolio selection, developed at Yale's YQuantum for the Capgemini and The Hartford challenge. We reduced 50 assets to 8 sectors mapped onto 8 qubits and applied QAOA to identify the optimal allocation under real regulatory constraints.

Circuit-depth optimizations roughly halved the depth and raised simulated accuracy from 17% to 53%, and we validated the result against a brute-force classical solve. Placed 2nd of 35 teams and won Best Computational Accuracy.
