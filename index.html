Yale Hackathon

2nd place

A quantum optimizer for insurance portfolios, built over a weekend at Yale's YQuantum for the Capgemini and Hartford challenge. The task: given a pile of assets and a set of rules, pick the best mix. We took 50 real assets, boiled them down to 8 sectors mapped onto 8 qubits, and used QAOA to search for the answer.

To make it run well we reworked the circuit so it was about half as deep, which pushed our simulated accuracy from 17% up to 53%. We also checked the quantum result against a brute-force solve to prove it was landing on the real optimum. 2nd of 35 teams, and Best Computational Accuracy.

Wind Tunnel (NACA 2415)

Spring 2026

Tested a NACA 2415 airfoil in a subsonic wind tunnel to see how it behaves as you tilt it. I read the surface pressures at seven angles of attack, then worked through the numbers in Python to pull out lift and moment and compared them against the textbook panel-method predictions and published NACA data. Two of the pressure taps were reading garbage, so part of the job was noticing that and cleaning it up without throwing off the results.
